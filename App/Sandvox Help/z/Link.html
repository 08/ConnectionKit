Link

Links are used to connect the pages of your site together. Both images and almost any text in a site can be made into a link. A link can be to another page in your site, or to an external page.

The Link Destination Panel

To work with a link, use the link inspector:


To link to an external page, choose the 'External page' option and type the address of the page into the box. To link to another page of your site, choose the 'Page in this Site' option and drag the icon to the page in the Site Outline.

Check the "Open link in new window" box to open the link in a new window.

When you click on a link while working on your site in Sandvox, it does not open. This allows you to edit the text that makes up the link. If you do want to follow the link, hold down the Shift key while clicking the link.

Creating, Editing and Removing Links

To create a link:

  • Select some text and then do one of the following:
    • Click the "Create Link" item in the toolbar.
    • Choose "Create Link…" from the Edit menu.

or:

  • Drag one of the following into a text area:
    • A bookmark from the Media Browser.
    • A URL from another application such as Safari.

If you have it set in your Preferences to "read URLs from web browser", Sandvox will automatically set any new links to point to the page you are currently viewing in Safari, Opera, Camino, Firefox, or OmniWeb.

To edit an existing link:

  1. Click part of the text surrounding the link to begin editing it.
  2. Click or drag the mouse to select part of the link.
  3. Click the "Edit Link" item in the toolbar or choose "Edit Link…" from the Edit menu.

The color of links is part of the site's design; you cannot change it.

To remove an existing link:

  1. Click part of the text surrounding the link to begin editing it.
  2. Click or drag the mouse to select part of the link.
  3. Click the "Edit Link" item in the toolbar or choose "Edit Link…" from the Edit menu.
  4. Choose 'nothing' from the 'Link To' pop up menu.

E-mail Links

To create a link to an e-mail address:

  1. Type the full e-mail address (for example, me@mywebsite.com).
  2. Select the address.
  3. Click the "Create Link" item in the toolbar. Sandvox automatically interprets the text to be an e-mail address.

So, for example with the address me@mywebsite.com, Sandvox automatically sets the link's address to be mailto:me@mywebsite.com. When a visitor clicks on the link, their e-mail client will begin a new message to the address.

Note that whenever you put an email address into the text of a web page, the e-mail address may be picked up by spammers and other unscrupulous people who use automated systems to find e-mail addresses. An alternative is to use a Contact Form instead to provide more protection.

Link Color

Unlike other text, you cannot change the color of a link. The color of a link is determined by the site's design. You cannot change this color without modifying the design itself. To do so requires a degree of CSS knowledge, and is not recommended for the majority of our users. If you do have the required knowledge, have a look at our Designer's Guide article on the subject.
