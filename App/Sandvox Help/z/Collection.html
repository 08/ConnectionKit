Collection

A collection "contains" other pages, much like a folder on your computer contains various files. You can nest as many collections within each other as you like.

Unlike a computer folder, however, a collection also contains content itself. Usually the main content is an index listing the items in that collection. (This page, when uploaded to your host, is usually called "index.html" but that file name is generally hidden from the person viewing your website.)

Every site has at least one collection, since the Home Page is actually a collection that contains all the other pages of the site.

A collection in the Site Outline.

To create a new collection:

  1. Click the "New" item in the toolbar or choose "New Page" from the Insert menu.
  2. Choose a blog or photo album to create a new collection.

To group existing pages into a new collection:

  1. Select one or more pages in the Site Outline.
  2. Click the "Group" item in the toolbar or choose "Group as General Collection" from the Insert menu.

Collection Settings

To edit a collection's settings:

  1. Click the collection in the Site Outline to select it.
  2. Open the Page Inspector then click the 'collection' tab.

Sorting

Select the order in which the pages of the collection are sorted:

None
You can arrange the order of the pages yourself in the Site Outline.
Alphabetical or reverse alphabetical
Pages are sorted in alphabetical order by their Page Title, either from A-Z or from Z-A.
Date Created
Sorts the pages by their date and time. You can sort with the most recent page at the top or bottom.
Date Modified
Sorts the pages by their date and time. You can sort with the most recent page at the top or bottom.

RSS Feed

Generates an RSS feed for the contents of the collection. Visitors can then subscribe to the RSS feed to be notified when you update the collection. The are several different options.

  • None
  • Standard
  • Photocast
  • Podcast

Most Recent Articles

The maximum number of pages to display in the RSS Feed.

Article Length

Slide the slider to the left or right to lengthen or shorten the length of the article that shows on the RSS Feed.

Publish Archives

If you are going to be using a collection archive you will need to check this box.

Blog Post Settings

To edit a blog's settings:

  1. Click the collection in the Site Outline to select it.
  2. Click the text of the blog post to select it.
  3. Open the Object Inspector.

Collection to index

Here you can drag the target to the main collection of your blog if it is not already done for you

Show at most

This is to set the number posts that will be seen on the main page of your collection. Useful for larger collections such as blogs where there may eventually be dozens of pages. Enter 0 for unlimited Index items.

Layout

There are many different 'views' to choose from, also you can check the options that you would like for your posts.

Article Length

Slide the slider to the left or right to lengthen or shorten the length of the article that shows on the main page of your collection.

Hyperlink titles

Check this box if you would like the title of your pages to be links to the pages.

Collection Presets

Collections can be used in many ways. Sandvox provides some pre-configured collections for the most popular uses. To use one of these presets, you can either:

  • Create a new collection by clicking the "New" item in the toolbar and select the type of page you want.
  • Select an existing collection in the Site Outline and use the Page Inspector to change its settings.


If you use one of these presets, don't forget that you can still tweak the settings of the collection using the Page Inspector. The following presets are available:

Blog
Designed for use as a Blog, Podcast or Videocast. Please see the "Blogging with Sandvox" and "Podcasting and Videocasting with Sandvox" articles for more information.
Photo Album
A grid of photos in the collection. Please see "Working with Photo Albums in Sandvox" for more information.
