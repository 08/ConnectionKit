Personal .Mac Domains

Apple's .Mac service has a feature known as ".Mac Personal Domain." This allows you to setup your own website domain (e.g. example.com) and host it using your .Mac account. Currently, Sandvox offers preliminary support for this feature via a hidden preference.

To set up your .Mac personal domain:

  1. Launch System Preferences.
  2. Go to the ".Mac" preference pane.
  3. Click the "Account Details" button under the "Account" tab.
  4. This should bring up the Apple .Mac login page. Login with your name and password.
  5. In the "Account Settings" page, click on "Personal Domain."
  6. Click the "Add Domain" and enter your domain (e.g. example.com) in both fields and click Continue.
  7. Go to your domain name registrar's web hosting area, and point your domain (by adding or changing a "CNAME record") to "web.mac.com".
    (You may need to contact your domain name registrar for help with this.)

After your domain is set up to point to your mac.com account — this can take up to 48 hours — you will be able to publish and view your website.

To set up Sandvox to publish to your .Mac personal domain:

  1. Quit Sandvox.
  2. Launch the Terminal application, which can be found in the "Applications → Utilities" folder.
  3. Type in the following command (replace "www.example.com" with your personal domain) and hit enter: 
    defaults write com.karelia.Sandvox DotMacPersonalDomain www.example.com
  4. Quit the Terminal application and relaunch Sandvox. Open your site document.
  5. Click "Setup Host" in the toolbar.
  6. Proceed through the Host Setup Assistant as you normally would for publishing to .Mac.
  7. When finished, choose "Publish Entire Site" from the Site menu.

Note that the verification stage (Step 5 of the connection test) will fail if you have not yet set up your personal domain to point to your website, or if the changes have not yet taken effect. Your website can still be viewed at http://web.mac.com/username/, however.

Please note that websites published to .Mac personal domains are placed in a different location in your iDisk than websites published to a normal .Mac publishing setup. If you have published a website from Sandvox without using this technique, it will still be stored in your iDisk's "Sites" folder, and be published at http://homepage.mac.com/username/.
